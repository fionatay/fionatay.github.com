Diversity & Inclusion Advocacy

I have a background in Chemistry and Physics, and thought I wanted to be a scientist as a child. In 2009, I learnt to program after taking CS 101 at Harvey Mudd College, because of the department's initiatives to make CS more welcoming to newbies :D

Read more about me on this Channel NewsAsia (Singapore) piece on representation in tech.

I'm committed to making the tech industry more inclusive. Some of my favorite organizations are CODE2040 and Write/Speak/Code. I've also worked with Women Who Code. Some of my materials:

  • I ran a workshop at Grace Hopper in 2012 on starting a women-in-CS group at colleges. Proposal here
  • I started #DisruptTechInterviews, a hashtag about how we need to revolutionize the interview process for software engineers. Read the Storify here
  • I've written about intersectionality and inclusion on the topic of #RealDiversityNumbers, the philosophy that diversity goes beyond % of women. Read the Storify
  • I think it's important for us to pay everyone fairly (per #talkpay) and was interviewed by Susie Cagle for the Guardian and was featured in The Verge.
